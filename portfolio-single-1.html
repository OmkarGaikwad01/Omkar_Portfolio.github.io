20 DOF Humonoid Hand

(Simulation lead)

Project Date FALL 2022
Role Simulation, Design, Coading
University Arizona State University

Project Description: Developed an interactive application using MATLAB to simulate and analyze a 20 Degree of Freedom (DoF) Humanoid Hand, showcasing kinematics fundamentals. Achievements: Successfully created a simulation application enabling comprehensive analysis of hand configurations. Demonstrated the intricate movement capabilities of a 20 DoF Robotic Hand through the application. Contribution: Led the team in MATLAB coding, app development, and error resolution, demonstrating leadership and technical prowess. Collaborated effectively to modify code, develop the app, and analyze hand gestures, significantly contributing to project success.

Future Work: Plan to enhance the application by integrating velocity and acceleration analysis features. Aim to enrich the Sign library to augment the educational value of the application. References: Utilized resources such as the Syngrasp toolbox documentation and "Robotics: Modelling, Planning, and Control" by Siciliano et al., showcasing a robust foundation in robotics. Team Collaboration: Worked cohesively with team members, leveraging individual strengths and skills to accomplish project objectives efficiently. Technical Skills: Demonstrated proficiency in MATLAB coding, app development, Machine Learning, kinematics fundamentals, and robotic hand movement analysis.
